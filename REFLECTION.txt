REFLECTION: GRAB & GO - LOOT ON THE RUN
Harriet Maleck - ST10252836
25 March 2026

Developing "Grab & Go: Loot on the Run" during this three-day game jam provided valuable insights into AI-assisted game development and iterative problem-solving. The theme "You are not the main character" challenged me to create a dual-character system where the player supports an AI-controlled thief rather than directly controlling the protagonist.

The most significant technical challenge involved implementing a reliable collectible pickup system. Initial attempts resulted in coins triggering from unrealistic distances (19+ units) due to oversized mesh colliders on imported prefabs. Through systematic debugging and collision analysis, I learned to override problematic prefab configurations programmatically, replacing them with properly-sized trigger colliders. This taught me the importance of validating third-party assets and implementing defensive coding practices.

AI assistance proved valuable for rapid prototyping and debugging complex trigger interactions. However, I quickly learned that AI-generated solutions required thorough testing and often needed multiple iterations to achieve the desired gameplay feel. The process reinforced the importance of understanding underlying systems rather than blindly implementing suggestions.

The project successfully demonstrates the core survival loop: timer pressure, collectible rewards, health management, and status feedback through a comprehensive HUD system. While features like tear gas resources and progressive difficulty remain unimplemented, the foundation supports future expansion and aligns with the design document's vision.
