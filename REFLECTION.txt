Developing Grab & Go: Loot on the Run during this game jam highlighted both the strengths and limitations of AI-assisted development. AI tools such as Cursor IDE and ChatGPT significantly accelerated prototyping, allowing rapid implementation of systems such as lane movement, collectibles, and the HUD. This enabled more time for refining gameplay and design decisions (Cursor IDE, n.d.; ChatGPT, n.d.).

However, challenges arose when AI-generated solutions were implemented without full understanding. For example, issues with collectible pickups revealed problems with collider configurations from third-party assets, reinforcing the need for critical evaluation and debugging (Unity Technologies, n.d.). This experience emphasized that AI should support, rather than replace, technical understanding.
AI also influenced my workflow by shifting development into a more iterative and conversational process. While this improved efficiency, it required constant validation to ensure accuracy and alignment with design goals.

Overall, the project demonstrates that AI can enhance productivity and problem-solving, but responsible use requires transparency, critical thinking, and a balance between assistance and independent learning.
